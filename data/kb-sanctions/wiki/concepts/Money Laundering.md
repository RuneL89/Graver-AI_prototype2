# Money Laundering

## Summary

The process of concealing the origins of money obtained through illegal means by passing it through a complex sequence of banking transfers or commercial transactions.

## Details

Money laundering typically involves three stages:
1. **Placement**: Introducing illicit cash into the financial system
2. **Layering**: Moving funds through multiple transactions, accounts, or jurisdictions to obscure their source
3. **Integration**: Reintroducing the laundered money into the legitimate economy as apparently clean income

Jens Hansen was sanctioned on 2025-11-14 for alleged involvement in both [[procurement fraud]] and money laundering (source: sanctions_list_march_2026.pdf). The sanctions list specifically notes his links to "offshore shell companies" — a classic layering mechanism used to hide the beneficial ownership of funds (source: sanctions_list_march_2026.pdf).

In the context of procurement fraud, money laundering often works as follows:
- A fraudulent contract is awarded to a vendor (e.g., PC-2025-00991)
- Payments are made to the vendor's account
- Funds are transferred to shell companies or offshore accounts
- The original criminal origin of the money is obscured
- Directors or officials extract the funds as personal wealth

The combination of procurement fraud and money laundering allegations against Hansen suggests investigators believe he did not merely manipulate contracts, but actively extracted and concealed proceeds.
