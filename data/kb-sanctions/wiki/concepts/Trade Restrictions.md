# Trade Restrictions

## Summary

Government-imposed limitations on the import, export, or transit of goods, services, or technology, often used as foreign policy or security tools.

## Details

Trade restrictions include sanctions, embargoes, export controls, and import bans. Violations occur when individuals or companies circumvent these controls — for example, by shipping goods through third countries, falsifying documentation, or using shell companies to hide the true end-user.

[[Global Shipping Partners]] was sanctioned on 2025-09-02 specifically for a "trade restrictions violation" (source: sanctions_list_march_2026.pdf). Unlike Jens Hansen, whose sanctions relate to procurement fraud and money laundering, Global Shipping Partners' designation is tied to the movement of goods across borders.

Common trade restriction violations in shipping and logistics include:
- Transporting sanctioned goods (arms, dual-use technology, controlled commodities)
- Providing services to sanctioned entities or countries
- Falsifying country-of-origin or end-user documentation
- Using transshipment through non-sanctioned jurisdictions to mask the true destination

Because Global Shipping Partners does not appear in the domestic business registry or procurement data, it may operate primarily in international waters or under foreign flags. Journalists should investigate whether [[Oceanic Logistics Ltd]] — a domestic logistics company — has any contractual or operational ties to Global Shipping Partners.
