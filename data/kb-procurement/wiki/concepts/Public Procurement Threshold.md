# Public Procurement Threshold

## Summary

Financial limits above which public contracts require competitive bidding, independent evaluation, and enhanced disclosure.

## Details

Public procurement thresholds are legal value limits that trigger specific procedural requirements for government contracts. When a contract exceeds the threshold, the awarding authority must typically follow open competitive bidding, publish award notices, and justify the selection (source: procurement_contracts_2025.pdf).

In the 2025 procurement dataset, both major contracts exceed typical thresholds:
- **PC-2025-00442** (Port Infrastructure Upgrade): USD 4,200,000 — far above any standard threshold
- **PC-2025-00991** (Government IT Services): USD 1,850,000 — also well above typical limits

These values should have triggered:
1. Open advertisement of the tender
2. Independent evaluation panel
3. Publication of award rationale
4. Disclosure of beneficial ownership of bidding companies
5. Cooling-off period for appeals

Journalists should verify whether these steps were actually followed. The absence of competitive bidding documentation for contracts of this size is a major [[red flag]] for [[procurement fraud]] or [[bid rigging]].
