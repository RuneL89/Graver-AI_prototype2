# Conflict of Interest

## Summary

A situation where an individual's personal interests compromise their ability to act impartially in an official capacity.

## Details

A conflict of interest arises when a person has competing loyalties or personal stakes that could influence their professional judgment. In public procurement, common conflicts include:
- Directors who hold positions in both bidding and awarding entities
- Family relationships between evaluators and bidders
- Financial interests in competing companies
- Undisclosed side agreements or kickbacks

In the current dataset, Jens Hansen is registered as a director of both Oceanic Logistics Ltd and NorthStar Procurement Inc (source: business_registry_q1_2026.pdf). Both companies won significant government contracts in 2025:
- Oceanic Logistics Ltd: PC-2025-00442 (USD 4,200,000)
- NorthStar Procurement Inc: PC-2025-00991 (USD 1,850,000)

This dual directorship creates a clear conflict of interest. If Hansen influenced the operations or bidding strategies of both companies, there could not have been genuine arm's-length competition between them. Additionally, Hansen was later sanctioned for [[procurement fraud]] and [[money laundering]], compounding the concern (source: sanctions_list_march_2026.pdf).
