# Corporate Transparency

## Summary

The principle that corporate ownership, control, and financial information should be accessible to regulators, journalists, and the public.

## Details

Corporate transparency is a foundational requirement for accountable markets and effective anti-corruption enforcement. Key transparency mechanisms include public business registries, beneficial ownership disclosures, audited financial statements, and procurement disclosure rules (source: business_registry_q1_2026.pdf).

The Q1 2026 business registry provides basic transparency — company names, registration numbers, directors, and addresses — but it lacks detailed beneficial ownership information (source: business_registry_q1_2026.pdf). The presence of aliases like "Director Y" in the registry demonstrates a gap: the registry captures legal directors but may not reveal who truly controls the company.

Journalists can use corporate transparency data to:
- Map networks of related companies through shared directors or addresses
- Identify conflicts of interest in government contracting
- Trace assets that have been moved between entities
- Flag individuals or companies that appear across multiple databases
