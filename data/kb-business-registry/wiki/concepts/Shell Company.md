# Shell Company

## Summary

A corporate entity without significant assets or operations, often used as a vehicle to obscure ownership or move funds.

## Details

A shell company is a non-operating business entity that typically exists only on paper. While not inherently illegal, shell companies are frequently exploited to hide [[beneficial ownership]], evade taxes, launder money, or circumvent sanctions (source: sanctions_list_march_2026.pdf).

In the current dataset, the sanctions list mentions that Jens Hansen is linked to "offshore shell companies" in addition to NorthStar Procurement Inc (source: sanctions_list_march_2026.pdf). This suggests that the corporate network around Hansen may extend beyond the entities visible in the domestic business registry.

Investigative red flags for shell companies include:
- Use of nominee directors or aliases (e.g., "Director Y")
- Shared addresses across multiple unrelated companies
- Lack of substantive business activity
- Registration in secrecy jurisdictions
