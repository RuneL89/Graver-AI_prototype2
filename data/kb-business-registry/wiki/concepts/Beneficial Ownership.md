# Beneficial Ownership

## Summary

The right to enjoy benefits from ownership even when legal title is held in another name. Central to tracing hidden control in corporate structures.

## Details

Beneficial ownership refers to the natural person(s) who ultimately own, control, or benefit from a company or asset, regardless of whose name appears on legal documents (source: business_registry_q1_2026.pdf). In investigative journalism, uncovering beneficial ownership is critical because shell companies and nominee directors are commonly used to obscure the true parties behind transactions.

In the current dataset, Jens Hansen is registered as a director of both Oceanic Logistics Ltd and NorthStar Procurement Inc (source: business_registry_q1_2026.pdf). In the Oceanic Logistics entry, he is listed as "Director Y (alias Jens Hansen)" — a red flag that suggests deliberate obfuscation of his identity (source: business_registry_q1_2026.pdf). This pattern is consistent with attempts to hide beneficial ownership or control.

The [[Financial Action Task Force (FATF)]] recommends that jurisdictions maintain beneficial ownership registries to prevent money laundering and sanctions evasion. The absence of a clear beneficial ownership declaration for either Oceanic Logistics Ltd or NorthStar Procurement Inc represents a significant transparency gap.
